# Why now

## Three catalysts converging in a narrow window

The pitch requires a crisp "why now" or VCs will assume the market was there before and someone already won it. Three forces hit simultaneously in 2023–2024 and create the opening.

## 1. Regulatory compounding

MoCRA is the lead catalyst but not the only one. The broader pattern is a regulatory surface that has been compounding across physical supply chains for a decade and finally reached the tipping point where mid-market manufacturers can no longer absorb it with spreadsheets.

- **MoCRA** (2022, enforced mid-2024). First federal cosmetic regulation since 1938. Mandatory facility registration, product listing, safety substantiation, adverse event reporting.
- **FSMA 204** (2023 rule finalized, 2026 compliance). Food Traceability Rule requires detailed record-keeping on high-risk foods.
- **DSCSA** (enforcement stabilized 2024). Drug Supply Chain Security Act — interoperable electronic tracing across pharma distribution.
- **California SB-343 and Prop 65 expansions.** State-level additions to labeling and disclosure obligations.
- **EU CSRD and CBAM** (2024–2026). For any US manufacturer with European retail exposure, ESG disclosure and carbon border adjustments become supply-chain obligations.
- **Retailer-driven overlays.** Sephora Clean, Ulta Conscious Beauty, Credo Clean Standard, Target Clean, Whole Foods Premium Body Care. Private-label compliance stacks on top of federal regulation.

The cumulative effect: mid-market manufacturers now spend 4–8% of revenue on compliance-adjacent activity, up from 1–3% a decade ago. That's the buying budget we unlock.

## 2. AI-native extraction as an unlock

Three years ago, this product was a JIRA backlog. The bottleneck was that supplier evidence arrives as unstructured PDFs, images of scanned letters, emails with inline attachments, SharePoint folders with ad-hoc naming conventions. Any pre-LLM attempt to build this product ended in a custom OCR pipeline, a services revenue model, and an implementation timeline that mid-market buyers couldn't absorb.

Modern LLMs solve the extraction step at 92–97% accuracy on our evidence types with a human-in-the-loop review floor at 75% confidence. The economics changed in 2023. The product shape we can ship today — AI-first extraction, operator review only on the fuzzy edges, first facility onboarded in two weeks — was not buildable in 2021.

Incumbents (Veeva, MasterControl) are still running legacy workflows designed around clean structured input. They'll retrofit AI, but the retrofit fights their existing product surface. A new entrant can go AI-native on day one.

## 3. Buyer pain at a new level

Two retail-side pressures bit in 2024 and the pain showed up in mid-market CMO P&Ls.

**Retailer consolidation and compression.** Sephora, Ulta, and Target's clean beauty aisles took a larger share of the indie brand market, which is where most mid-market CMOs make their margin. Those retailers enforce stricter vendor compliance programs than the regional independent retailers they displaced. The bar is higher, the chargebacks bigger, the documentation requirements more structured. A CMO that used to handle this with a two-person Compliance Ops team suddenly can't.

**Audit frequency increased.** Brand-side QA teams responded to MoCRA enforcement by pulling their supplier audit cycles from biennial to quarterly. Mid-market CMOs that used to face four audits a year now face twelve. The workload tripled. The team didn't. The software didn't.

**Chargeback accrual visibility.** Finance teams at mid-market CMOs started treating compliance chargebacks as a monitored line item in monthly close in 2023–2024, which means the CFO now sees the pain the operator has been absorbing for years. The budget for a compliance workflow tool moved from "maybe next year" to "this quarter."

## The narrow window

All three forces will continue to intensify, but the entrant window has a clock. Vanta will extend horizontally at some point. Veeva Vault is plausibly three years from a mid-market product. One of the ERP bolt-on vendors will cobble together a MoCRA module. The window to establish the evidence graph and the operator workflow as the default surface is 18–36 months.

Capital now buys the right to own the default. Capital in 2027 buys the chance to be the number-two vendor in a defined category.

## How to say this in the pitch

One slide, three bullets, no more. Regulation compounding. AI unlock in 2023. Mid-market pain now on the P&L. Then move on. The temptation with "why now" is to list ten catalysts and make them all feel equally important. The discipline is to pick the three that a VC can repeat back to you ninety minutes later.
