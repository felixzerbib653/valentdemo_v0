# Thesis — distilled

## 30 seconds

Valent Trust is a continuous-compliance layer for regulated physical supply chains. We start with cosmetic contract manufacturers (CMOs), where MoCRA just made supplier evidence a survival-level obligation and every retailer audit is still a three-week archaeological dig across SharePoint and Outlook. We turn that chaos into a live trust grid — seven pillars of evidence, per supplier, per ingredient, scored and monitored — and produce a one-click audit bundle that Compliance Ops sends to retailers instead of their resume. Vanta for suppliers, built for the operator.

## 2 minutes

Mid-market CMOs ($10–$250M revenue, 2–20 facilities) live in a supplier-evidence nightmare. A single brand audit pulls on 80–200 suppliers, each with a dozen documents — FEI registrations, safety substantiations, allergen letters, origin attestations, purity specs. Today that evidence lives in inboxes and file shares. The Compliance Ops operator (Sarah, in our personas) spends 60% of her week chasing suppliers for documents she already has somewhere, rebuilding the same audit bundle she built three months ago, and missing renewals that become retailer chargebacks.

MoCRA — the first federal cosmetic manufacturing regulation since 1938 — made this acute. §606 FEI registration, §607 product listing, and §609 safety substantiation are now federal obligations with rolling deadlines. Retailers are demanding attestation. FDA enforcement is new, so no one knows the hot stove — which is exactly the pressure that creates buying urgency.

We sell a daily workflow, not an IT project. Operators open Valent at 9am, see the trust grid, triage the blocked suppliers, draft chase emails in one click, and produce audit bundles in under 60 seconds. The artifact is the viral loop: every bundle gets forwarded to a retailer auditor, an FDA inspector, or a new procurement partner, and that's how Valent gets discovered in adjacent accounts.

The wedge is compliance. The expansion is bidding intelligence (Year 2 — per-supplier trust data becomes deal data) and margin recovery (Year 3 — per-ingredient costing and substitution sandbox). The spine is the evidence graph. Everything else hangs off it.

## 10 minutes

Open with the operator. Sarah at Elevation Labs, a 2-facility mid-market CMO. Her Monday morning: 47 unread supplier emails, a retailer audit due Friday, and a feeling she's one missing FEI registration away from a chargeback. That's the ICP.

Move to the catalyst. MoCRA passed in 2022, hit compliance deadlines in 2024, and is still being enforced. Retailers (Sephora, Ulta, Target wellness aisles) are pulling trust data into their vendor scorecards. Mid-market CMOs are the squeezed middle — large enough to have 100+ suppliers, small enough that a single chargeback is existential. AI extraction is what makes this product possible now; three years ago, the evidence plane was too unstructured to be useful software.

Move to the product. Seven pillars, live monitoring, one-click bundles. Show the demo: Sarah opens the grid, sees two blocked suppliers, drafts a chase email with the missing-evidence list auto-filled, generates the audit bundle for the Friday retailer. 90 seconds, four clicks.

Move to the market. 600 cosmetic CMOs in the US, ~150 in ICP, $30–80k ACV — that's a $4.5–12M ARR wedge market. Small on paper. What makes the bet work is the expansion surface: dietary supplements (2x the CMO count, same regulatory shape), food additives (FSMA 204), industrial chemicals (REACH, TSCA). The AI-native compliance layer for regulated physical supply chains is a $2–4B TAM on a decade horizon.

Close on the moat. The evidence graph compounds: every supplier document ingested makes the next extraction faster and sharper. Per-vertical pillar definitions become regulatory surface knowledge that's hard to copy. Audit history creates operational lock-in — pulling two years of evidence out of Valent is a quarter-long migration. And the artifact is the network: once a supplier sees the Valent bundle format, they start producing evidence to match it, which makes the next CMO's onboarding faster.

The ask is the same as every seed pitch: capital to convert the 12 design-partner pipeline into 20 paying CMOs, prove the expansion path to supplements, and earn a Series A into the platform.
