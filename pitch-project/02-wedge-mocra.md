# The wedge — MoCRA

## Why compliance is the wedge (and not margin or bidding)

Compliance is the wedge because it's existential, non-negotiable, and calendar-driven. A CMO can defer margin optimization for a quarter. They cannot defer an FDA inspection. The buyer's decision surface is small when the pain is acute — and MoCRA enforcement made it acute in 2024.

Margin and bidding are the expansion, not the wedge, for three reasons. First, the buyer for margin optimization is the COO or CFO, not the Compliance Ops operator — different champion, different sales cycle, different proof. Second, the ROI claim on margin recovery is debatable (what's the counterfactual?) while the ROI on avoiding a retailer chargeback is a line item in last year's P&L. Third, leading with margin positions us against ERP consultants and pricing optimization vendors where we have no analogy to hang on; leading with compliance positions us next to Vanta, which is a category every investor understands in five seconds.

A previous product iteration opened the pitch with margin and it confused buyers, diluted the Vanta analogy, and made the expansion story feel like a bait-and-switch. We do not go back to that.

## Why MoCRA specifically

The Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act passed in December 2022 and is the first federal cosmetic manufacturing regulation since the 1938 Food Drug and Cosmetic Act. It introduced four obligations with real teeth:

- **§606 — Facility registration (FEI).** Every cosmetic manufacturing facility needs an active FDA Establishment Identifier, updated every two years.
- **§607 — Product listing.** Every cosmetic product must be listed with the FDA, with ingredient declarations.
- **§609 — Safety substantiation.** Responsible persons must maintain records of adequate safety substantiation for each cosmetic product.
- **Adverse event reporting.** Serious adverse events must be reported within 15 business days.

The original compliance deadlines were July 2024 (facility registration, product listing), with subsequent deadlines for adverse-event systems and safety substantiation. FDA extended some deadlines once, and enforcement began mid-2024. The regulatory surface is still settling, which is exactly the pressure that creates buying urgency — no one knows what a hot stove feels like yet, and no one wants to be the first test case.

## The CMO-specific twist

The "Responsible Person" under MoCRA is typically the brand, not the CMO. That's the legal position. The operational position is different: every brand's QA team turns to their contract manufacturer and says "prove to me you're MoCRA-compliant or I'll move the work." Retailers (Sephora, Ulta, Target's clean beauty programs, Whole Foods body care) layered their own trust requirements on top. The CMO, legally a step removed from the obligation, is in practice the one producing the evidence.

This asymmetry is our opening. CMOs can't duck the obligation but don't have the infrastructure the brands do. MasterControl and Veeva sell to the brands. No one is selling the operator-grade workflow to the CMO in the middle.

## Why MoCRA is a wedge and not a market

MoCRA-specific TAM is small. Maybe 2000 cosmetic manufacturing facilities in the US across contract and brand-owned, mostly small. If Valent were only a MoCRA compliance tool, we'd cap at $15–25M ARR. The reason MoCRA is the wedge and not the market is that the evidence graph we build for MoCRA generalizes — supplements (DSHEA + FSMA), food additives (FSMA 204, FDA Food Safety), industrial chemicals (REACH, TSCA), nutraceuticals, veterinary products. The regulatory shape is the same: fragmented source documents, pillar-based obligations, recurring attestation windows. The product shape is the same: ingest, extract, score, bundle.

MoCRA is the surface where the wedge fits. The product is the infrastructure underneath.
