# The artifact — audit bundle

## Why the bundle is the center of gravity

Every durable SaaS product has a hero artifact. Vanta has the SOC 2 report PDF. Carta has the cap table. Stripe has the receipt. Mercury has the wire confirmation. The artifact is the thing the user forwards — to an auditor, an investor, a customer, a board member — and it's the viral surface that teaches outsiders what the product does.

For Valent Trust, the artifact is the audit bundle. A PDF (or link-shareable folder) that contains:

- A cover page with the supplier name, facility address, trust score, bundle generation timestamp, and the Valent provenance breadcrumb (what extracted what, when, with what confidence).
- A seven-pillar evidence index with the ranked source documents for each pillar.
- The source documents themselves, normalized to readable form.
- A chain-of-custody trail for each document (source, ingest date, extraction confidence, operator review status).

Sarah sends this to a retailer auditor. The retailer auditor opens it, sees a professional, timestamped, structured artifact, and closes the request. Sarah's week just got 40 hours shorter.

## Why the bundle travels

Every audit creates a bundle. Every bundle gets opened by a third party — retailer QA, FDA inspector, brand compliance officer at the customer, new procurement partner onboarding the CMO. That third party sees the Valent brand, the provenance chip, and the cover-page format. Some fraction of them asks "what is this" and starts a conversation. Some fraction of that fraction becomes a design-partner lead.

This is the same dynamic that made Vanta's SOC 2 report a growth channel. Artifact-driven distribution compounds because every customer produces many artifacts per year, and every artifact reaches a new audience of potential buyers. We don't need to fund a field sales team for the first 40 customers if the bundle does the work.

## The bundle as proof of expansion

Past the wedge, the bundle is the evidence that the expansion arc is real. Year 1, the bundle is a compliance artifact sent to retailers. Year 2, the same bundle (with different program formats) becomes the bid-qualification attachment a CMO sends to a prospective brand — "here's our trust posture across your ingredient list." Year 3, the bundle becomes a per-ingredient sourcing report with cost and substitution data layered in, and procurement teams at brands consume it to make formulation decisions.

The artifact shape is the product's expansion ladder in physical form. Same export surface, progressively richer payload.

## What makes it credible

Three things in particular.

**Provenance.** Every field in the bundle is tagged with its source — which document, which page, which extraction run, what confidence. Auditors reward traceability. We build it in from the first row of the demo.

**Live timestamps.** "Last scan: 3 minutes ago." Freshness is the feature. Static compliance reports are the incumbent, and they're stale on arrival. Our bundle is a snapshot of a living record, and the cover page says so.

**Operator attribution on the human calls.** When a flag was resolved by a human ("resolved with note: supplier re-registered FEI on 2024-03-14, confirmed via email"), we say so. When Valent extracted a field, we attribute to Valent. The chain-of-custody is honest about what was machine and what was human. That honesty is what auditors and buyers need to trust it.

## What we don't do with the bundle

We don't let operators edit extracted fields without audit trail. We don't ship a bundle with documents below the confidence threshold without a human review gate. We don't let the bundle become a free-form PDF builder — every field is typed, every pillar structured, every export constrained to the seven-pillar schema (plus program-format overlays).

The discipline is what makes the artifact durable. A free-form PDF generator is a nice feature. A trust-graded audit bundle with enforced provenance is a moat.
